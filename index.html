What are Comics

Comics is a medium used to express ideas with images, often combined with text or other visual information. It typically takes the form of a sequence of panels of images. Textual devices such as speech balloons, captions, and onomatopoeia can indicate dialogue, narration, sound effects, or other information. There is no consensus among theorists and historians on a definition of comics; some emphasize the combination of images and text, some sequentiality or other image relations, and others historical aspects such as mass reproduction or the use of recurring characters. Cartooning and other forms of illustration are the most common means of image-making in comics. Photo comics is a form that uses photographic images. Common forms include comic strips, editorial and gag cartoons, and comic books. Since the late 20th century, bound volumes such as graphic novels, and comic albums, have become increasingly common, along with webcomics.

Different Types of Comic Books

There are different types of Comic Books as a medium just like there are different type of books. I am going to cover the most common types of Comic Books which are Single Issues Floppies, Trade Paperbacks/Compacts , and Omnibuses . The main difference between each different type of Comic is sizing. Ranging from smallest to lowest, we have Single Issues, Trade Paperbacks, and Omnibuses. Depending on the story they may have each type of comic book or just certain forms mostly dependant on current serilization.

Single Issues

Single Issue or Floppy your most standrad comic book that most people think about when they envision a comic book. You can think of Single Issue comics as a single chapter in a book. Commonly coming out weekly,biweekly, or monthly. Issues are the singular, magazine-style format that you see in comic book shops. They are typically 20-30 pages. There is also a special type of single issue called a Variant. When a comic book gets sold out and needs to be reprinted these companies typically reprint these comics with different covers done by different types of artists. Making each reprint of the same comic issue exciting the different types of art. Typically single issues have a very wide range in prices but standard MSRP is around $5-$10 dollars per single issue which can get really expensive when collecting. Non-standard pricing depends on variants because some people enjoy original covers and since they are out of production they can skyrocket as a vintage price for over $100+

Trade Paperbacks and Compacts

Trade Paperbacks are a collection of single issues into a single Volume. Each Volume usually either denotes the finishing of a story arc or story itself. Volumes are several issues bound together in a larger format. It takes longer for the volumes to release because you have to wait until you have enough single issues to justify printing one. There are different volume formats, the most typical one is a trade paperback. The other type of trade paperbacks are called Compacts. (Image Shown on Right) Compacts are just trade paperbacks but they are the size of your average book. So they are shorter and less wide than your typical trade paperback. Trade Paperbacks usually range between $15-$30 and Compacts usually costs less around the $10-$20 bracket.

Omnibus

A comic Omnibus or Compendium is an oversized, hardcover book that collects a large number of comic book issues into a single volume. Unlike smaller trade paperbacks (TPBs) that typically compile a single story arc, an omnibus often collects an entire series or a significant run by a particular writer or artist. An omnibus is basically a collection of comic book issues or story arcs all bundled up into one massive, jaw-dropping book. It is like the comic book version of an all-you-can-eat buffet, but instead of unlimited shrimp cocktails, you get an unlimited feast of awesome artwork and epic storytelling. So in other words you can call Omnibuses a collection of volumes or a large collection of single issues. For example some Omnibuses can contain between 5-6 Volumes or 30-40 Single Issues worth of content. These are usually significantly more expensive for an upfront cost for obvious reasons that it is a much larger book but usually it would condense to be a cheaper alternative than buying every single volume or every single issue contained within that Omnibus. Many of these range between $70 and $200.
