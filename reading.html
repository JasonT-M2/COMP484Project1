This section here focuses mainly on how to buy physical copies of comic books. Online and Physical to buy the various different types of comic books. I will also provide ways to read comic books online as more and more online ways are being made to make reading these amazing stories more accessible in our day and age.

DISCLAIMER I sadly do not provide piracy sites but I do provide a couple free methods

Local Comic Book Store

This is the best way to buy comics in my opinion. First it allows local communities to thrive, it is generally cheaper than buying online, and you don't give your money to greedy corporations Amazon . I do have to say this is the most intimidating option because it is quite an overwhelming experience to first enter and be surrounded by boxes and boxes of old school single issues or shelves and shelves of the latest volumes or issues. My tips is to not be afraid to be lost and ask questions about how the store is set up or have a list of stories you want to try so someone can help you find them. Every single comic book store has a different layout but they all have an amazing energy I would recommend trying to buy from one at least once. My personal favorite is not a local one for me but is House of Secrets out in Burbank (image shown on left).

Online Shopping

InstockTrades is the most popular online comic book provider among enthusiasts especially in selling Omnibuses/Compendiums. They typically have the cheapest online prices compared to others that aren't Amazon . I do have to say there website is quite bad in layout but once you find something you want or need than they are one of the best options. Another one that is popular is called PanelBoundComics they have a ok reputation and more branding than inStockTrades but I don't recommend it as much as inStockTrades. PanelBound does frequently run deals especially around movie releases so they are worth checking out especially if you are avoiding Amazon .

Ways to Read/Experience

So the two best legal ways is through Marvel and DC subscription based unlimited comic readers that allow you to access almost their entire catalog for around $8 a month.

  • Marvel Unlimited
  • DC Infinite

Not bad for a 100 years worth of stories and experiences with various different authors. Almost like a library subscription. Speaking of Library, I am unsure of how to fully do this but if you have a library card you can connect it to a library app that allows you to read the library's entire comic book collection which would be a free way to enjoy three stories. My preferred way that I've been able to consume these stories is through YouTube channels such as ComicStorian and Comics Explained go through entire stories that essentially give you these stories in audiobook form. You don't get the full experience by seeing every panel and many times these channels need to blur the speech bubbles but this was a good enough experience for me to fall in love with the medium of comic books. A Zero Cost Experience to have my entire life flipped around to be obsessed with these amazing stories.
